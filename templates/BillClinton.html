The President when you were born was...

Bill Clinton

Bill Clinton was president from January 20, 1993 to January 20, 2001. The first Democrat in the White House in over 12 years, Clinton was known for his centrist "Third Way" philosophy, enacting NAFTA, the controversial 1994 crime bill, welfare reform, and financial deregulation measures. Despite his party losing control of both chambers of Congress for the first time in four decades in 1994, Clinton himself comfortably won reelection in 1996. In his second term, Clinton became embroiled in the Monica Lewinsky scandal, which resulted in his eventual impeachment, but not conviction. The Clinton years saw unprecedented peace in global geopolitics and record economic prosperity at home, and in the last three years of his administration, the federal government posted a budget surplus for the first time since 1969. Clinton left office with some of the highest approval ratings ever for a president.
