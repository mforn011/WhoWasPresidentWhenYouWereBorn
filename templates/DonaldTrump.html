The President when you were born was...

Donald Trump

Donald Trump was president from January 20, 2017 to January 20, 2021. Despite having never held public office, Trump ran for president in 2016, with his campaign platform widely being described as populist, protectionist, isolationist, and nativist. During the campaign, Trump was condemned for making many controversial and outright false statements, both from within and outside his party. After an unexpected victory in the Republican primaries, Trump went on to defeat Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton in that year's election, although losing the popular vote, in one of the biggest upsets in the history of American politics. His presidency was marked by many controversial actions, including a Muslim travel ban, an attempt to build a large wall on the U.S.–Mexico border, and the separation of migrant families detained at the border. His administration was aggressively protectionist, withdrawing the U.S. from the TPP and initiating trade wars with several countries, including China. In the last year of his presidency, the world became mired in the COVID-19 pandemic, the worst in a century, and Trump was widely criticized for his response to the pandemic. In the run-up to the 2020 election, Trump spread numerous falsehoods and misinformation, and wrongly claimed the election had been rigged after losing. The encouragement of his supporters to force government officials to change the election results on Trump's part would eventually culminate in the January 6, 2021 Capitol attack.
